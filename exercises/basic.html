The Luminous World of Impressionism

Impressionism, an art movement that emerged in the 19th century, revolutionized painting by focusing on capturing the fleeting impressions of light, color, and atmosphere rather than precise detail. Artists like Claude Monet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, and Edgar Degas used visible brushstrokes, open composition, and an emphasis on light in its changing qualities to depict modern life and landscapes. Their innovative approach paved the way for modern art.
