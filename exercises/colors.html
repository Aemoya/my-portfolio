The Luminous World of Impressionism

Impressionism, an art movement that emerged in the 19th century, revolutionized painting by focusing on capturing the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere. Artists like Monet, Renoir, and Degas used visible brushstrokes, open composition, and vibrant colors to depict scenes of modern life and landscapes, often painted en plein air (outdoors). Their aim was not to depict a subject with photographic realism, but to convey the "impression" of a moment, emphasizing how light changes and interacts with colors.
